Layout
Entry door: top-right. Window + black tile: top wall (no accessories). Private lobby right of door (not shared): shoe cabinet + chair. Right wall inside: lift → changing room with mirror → curtain → fire door → storage. Left wall: mirror + quotes. Bottom: IKEA cabinet, back room (admin + your office), toilet.
Zone notes
Private lobby (not shared)
Yours alone. Shoe cabinet + chair. Reform. A-board or logo plate on wall, warm lighting. First impression — make it count.
Left wall — mirror + quotes
545cm full-height mirror. Vinyl decals: "You don't find strength. You reform it." at eye level facing reformers. "reset. reform. repeat." near entry end.
Changing room (right wall) — tag programme spot
Linen curtain panels · small bench · hooks at two heights · warm strip lighting above mirror. Quote here: "Pilates is hell. That's why you'd look devilish hot." Post-class selfie with this quote = most shareable content in the studio.
Ceiling — hide the ugly things
Fake ceiling with slatted wood panels or white plasterboard + recessed track lighting. Track lighting doubles as the key rig for reformer lineup photo moment.
Equipment / peg wall
Matte black peg rail + floating natural wood shelves. Ref: the black-peg + cream-shelf inspo you shared. Magic circles on pegs, balls + rollers on shelves. Keeps floor clean, photographs beautifully.
Back room
Your office. Clients never see inside. Keep door closed during class hours. Booking terminal, merch stock, desk, printer.

Grid rules
Colour ratio
~50% dark/ink posts · 25% sand/neutral · 25% blush+rosy accent. Never two blush posts side by side in the grid.
Typography in posts
Unbounded for brand marks and short words. DM Serif italic for quotes. Never mix both in one post.
Caption style
All lowercase. Short first line (hook). No excessive hashtags — max 5. Period at the end of brand statements ("reset. reform.")
What to avoid
Busy backgrounds. Pink-pink-pink in a row. Generic fitness stock photos. Canva templates that look like every other studio.
